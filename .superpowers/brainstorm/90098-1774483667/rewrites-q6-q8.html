"Something drops in my stomach — I'm already running through what I did wrong"
Bridge: "That feeling is your body caring before your brain catches up. Makes sense."
"I notice it — like a flicker — but I can let it pass"
Bridge: "That pause between noticing and reacting — most people never build that."
"Honestly? I'd move on to something else without thinking much about it"
Bridge: "Your nervous system knows how to protect your energy. That's not nothing."

Bridge: "I appreciate you putting that into your own words."
Changes: Peter asks about the body, not the mind → embodied, not clinical. "What happens in your body first?" uses presupposition (something DOES happen). Options describe sensations, not self-diagnoses.

Bridge: "When it sticks like that, it means it matters to you. That's not a flaw."
"I feel numb — like I've gone somewhere inside myself and I can't get back yet"

Bridge: "I appreciate you putting that into your own words."
Changes: "What does the next morning feel like?" grounds it in a real moment instead of asking for a self-assessment duration. Options describe felt experience. Dropped the 4-tier time scale (bounce back / few hours / day / shutdown) — those read like a clinical severity rating.
