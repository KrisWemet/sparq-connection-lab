Onboarding Rewrites — Q9, Q10

Before → After: The two most sensitive questions

Q9: Inner Voice — Before

"When things get rocky between you, what does the voice inside your head usually sound like?"

• "It says I'm probably the problem" • "It says we're both human and we'll figure it out" • "It wonders if they're losing interest in me" • "It gets pretty loud and hard to quiet" • Write my own
Q9: Inner Voice — After

"Everyone has a voice that shows up during a fight — even after it's over. What does yours tend to say?"

"You messed this up. Again."
Bridge: "That voice has been with you a long time, hasn't it. It's not the truth — but it feels like it."
"They're pulling away. You can feel it."
Bridge: "When that voice speaks, it's hard to hear anything else. That's something we can work with."
"This is hard, but you'll get through it together"
Bridge: "That voice is rare. Most people have to build it — sounds like you've already started."
"It's just noise — loud, swirling, no clear words"
Bridge: "When everything gets loud at once, clarity is the first thing to go. That's your nervous system, not your character."
Write my own
Bridge: "I appreciate you putting that into your own words."
Changes: "Everyone has a voice" normalizes the experience before asking. Options are written as direct quotes from the voice — visceral, not analytical. "You messed this up. Again." hits different than "It says I'm probably the problem." The user recognizes the voice, they don't categorize themselves.
Q10: Childhood Safety — Before

"Growing up — was home a place that felt mostly safe and steady for you?"

• "Mostly yes" • "It had its moments, but mostly okay" • "It was complicated" • "Not really — it was hard" • Write my own
⚠️ This reads like a trauma screening questionnaire. The 4-tier scale (safe → complicated → hard) maps directly to clinical severity. Too direct for a first session.
Q10: Childhood Safety — After

"The way we grew up shapes the way we love — whether we realize it or not. When you think about where you came from, what feels most true?"

"I had a pretty solid foundation — it gave me something to build on"
Bridge: "That's something to be grateful for. It shows up in how you show up."
"There's stuff I've had to unlearn — patterns I picked up without choosing them"
Bridge: "Seeing inherited patterns for what they are — that's already the first step past them."
"I carry more from that time than I'd like to"
Bridge: "Thank you for trusting me with that. What you carry doesn't define where you're headed."
Write my own
Bridge: "I appreciate you putting that into your own words."
Changes: Complete reframe. Peter leads with why this matters ("shapes the way we love") instead of asking a direct safety question. No mention of "safe," "hard," or "home." Options are about relationship to the past — "stuff I've had to unlearn," "patterns I picked up" — which gives the same trauma signal without asking "was your childhood bad?" Dropped from 4 to 3 options. Bridges point forward, not back.
