Onboarding Rewrites — Q11, Q13, Q14

Bridges fix, life context reframe, and splitting the double-duty question

Q11: Conflict Style — Before

"When you and [partner] hit a rough patch, which sounds most like you?"

• "I bring it up — even when it's uncomfortable" • "I need space to cool down before I can talk" • "I try to make sure we both feel heard" • Write my own
Options are okay but read like a personality quiz. Bridges are good though — this one mostly needs the question reframed.
Q11: Conflict Style — After

(uses partnerName) "Think about the last real disagreement you and [partner] had. Not the topic — just what you did. Which is closest?"

"I said what I was feeling — probably before I was ready to say it well"
Bridge: "That instinct to speak even when it's messy — it means the silence scares you more than the conflict. That tells me something."
"I left the room — or at least went quiet until the charge wore off"
Bridge: "Leaving to come back calmer — that takes more discipline than people give it credit for."
"I tried to hear their side first, even while I was upset"
Bridge: "Holding space while you're hurting — that's a muscle. And you've clearly been building it."
Write my own
Bridge: "I appreciate you putting that into your own words."
Changes: "Not the topic — just what you did" grounds it in a real memory. Options describe actions with honest imperfection ("probably before I was ready to say it well") instead of clean self-descriptions.
Q13: Life Context — Before

"Last check-in before we really get going. What's life feeling like right now, outside the relationship?"

• "Pretty steady — things are okay" • "Busy and a bit stretched thin" • "We're going through a big change right now" • "It's been heavy — loss, grief, or something really hard" • Write my own
Not bad, but "loss, grief, or something really hard" feels like a checklist. And the question could use more pull.
Q13: Life Context — After

"Almost there. Outside your relationship — how much room do you have right now? Like, honestly."

"Things are calm enough — I've got bandwidth for this"
Bridge: "Good — that means we can go a little deeper when you're ready."
"I'm juggling a lot — but I'm here because this matters"
Bridge: "Showing up when you're stretched — that's not nothing. We'll keep it light and doable."
"Honestly, life is a lot right now"
Bridge: "Then we start with what's manageable and build from there. No pressure to be anywhere you're not."
Write my own
Bridge: "I appreciate you putting that into your own words."
Changes: "How much room do you have" is more useful than "what's life feeling like" — it tells us about capacity, not just mood. Collapsed 4 options to 3 (transition + heavy merged into "life is a lot right now"). Bridges set expectations for pacing.
Q14: Growth Goal + Check-in Frequency — Before

"And what is it you're really hoping for — the thing you can't quite put words to yet, but you feel it?"

Free text input for growth goal, then:
• Every day · A few times a week · Once a week
⚠️ Two problems: (1) "the thing you can't quite put words to" may freeze users who aren't ready for that depth. (2) Check-in frequency jammed into the same question kills the emotional moment.
Q14: Growth Goal — After (split into Q14a + Q14b)
Q14a: Growth Goal

"Last one. If something shifted in your relationship over the next few weeks — something real — what would that look like for you?"

Free text input
Bridge (after they type): "That's worth building toward. I'll remember that."
Q14b: Check-in Frequency (separate beat)

"One practical thing — how often do you want to show up here?"

"Every day — I want to build a real habit"
Bridge: "Five minutes a day changes more than you'd think. Let's do it."
"A few times a week — enough to keep it going"
Bridge: "Consistency beats intensity. A few times a week is plenty."
"Once a week to start — let me ease in"
Bridge: "Starting where you are is always the right pace."
Changes: Split into two beats. Q14a asks for the goal with a concrete presupposition — "if something shifted... what would that look like?" — not "the thing you can't put words to." Users can answer "we'd laugh more" or "I'd stop walking on eggshells" — no deep excavation required. Q14b is a clean practical question with its own energy. The emotional close (growth goal) and the logistical close (frequency) each get their own moment.
Bonus: Q2 Bridge Fix

Age options: No bridge needed — just flow to pronouns seamlessly

These are demographic questions — Peter shouldn't over-validate them. Short and clean is right. The real warmth comes in the questions that matter.
