Q10: Childhood — Take 2

Less self-aware, more felt experience

Problem with v1
"Patterns I picked up without choosing them" and "stuff I've had to unlearn" require self-awareness most users won't have yet. That's the language of someone who's already done the work.
Q10 v2 — Felt experience, no self-awareness required

"The way we grew up shapes the way we love — whether we realize it or not. When you think about home growing up, what comes up?"

"Mostly good memories — I felt like I belonged there"
Bridge: "That kind of ground under your feet — it carries forward into everything."
"A mix — some good, some things I'd rather not repeat"
Bridge: "Most people land here. The fact that you can see both sides says a lot."
"It was hard — I learned early to take care of myself"
Bridge: "You built something out of that. We'll make sure it works for you here, not against you."
Write my own
Bridge: "I appreciate you putting that into your own words."
What changed from v1: Options are now things anyone could say — "I felt like I belonged," "some things I'd rather not repeat," "I learned early to take care of myself." No therapy vocabulary. The hardest option ("I learned early to take care of myself") carries the same trauma signal as the old "Not really — it was hard" but frames it through resilience, not deficit.
