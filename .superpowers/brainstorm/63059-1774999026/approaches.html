Three Approaches to a Revolutionary Sparq Redesign

All three accept: warm palette stays, Peter is a coach not a companion, success = ritual → self-awareness → relationship change

A

The Practice Arc

Core premise: Real behavior change needs a trigger, not just an action. Right now Peter sends users INTO the app. The redesign sends users INTO the world.

What changes:

  • Morning becomes a 3-screen mission brief: Why → What → When (user sets their own trigger moment)
  • The action happens in real life. No "Did you do this today?" checkbox — evening opens with "Tell me about your trigger moment"
  • Evening is a debrief, not a reflection. Peter asks the coach's question: "What did you notice that surprised you?"
  • The daily loop becomes a lived arc (morning brief → real world → evening debrief)
  • Peter's voice is in every word of the brief — not chatting, but coaching

What stays: Journeys, warm design, Peter avatar at key moments, session structure

Rooted in implementation intentions research — the most evidence-backed behavior change technique

B

The Body-First Loop

Core premise: The body tracks truth that the mind narrativizes. Starting each session in the body creates emotional honesty before any content is consumed.

What changes:

  • Every session opens with a 4-state somatic check (not words — a 2×2 grid: open/closed × heavy/light)
  • Peter reads the body state and adjusts the day's practice in real time — heavy day gets a permission practice, energized day gets a depth practice
  • Body state over 30 days IS the growth visualization — not a streak counter, a mood terrain map
  • Evening closes with same 4-state check: "How does your body feel now?" — the delta is the data
  • Wearable integration possible (but not required)

What stays: Journey content, Peter voice, warm design, 5-minute sessions

Rooted in somatic psychology + ACT values work — brings the body into a currently mind-only app

C

The Identity Mirror

Core premise: Sustainable change happens when people's actions align with who they believe they're becoming. The app's job is to make that becoming visible.

What changes:

  • The central object is your Identity Arc — a living statement that evolves as you practice ("I'm becoming someone who pauses before reacting → who responds from values → who creates safety for my partner")
  • Every session is in service of the arc. Peter frames the morning brief as a chapter in your story, not a daily task
  • Growth is visible as a narrative timeline ("Day 1: noticed the pattern. Day 7: paused once. Day 14: did it without thinking.")
  • Identity arc is the primary dashboard object — replacing streak, score, and journey arc
  • Peter coaches identity shifts, not behaviors: "You're not just communicating better — you're becoming someone your partner can be honest with"

What stays: Journey content, warm design, Peter as anchor, 5-minute structure

Rooted in Narrative Therapy + Identity-Based Habits (Atomic Habits research) — the most powerful long-term change lever

My recommendation: A + C combined. The Practice Arc gives users a behavioral mechanism that actually works (implementation intentions). The Identity Mirror gives them a reason to keep going (visible becoming). B (body) is powerful but raises scope — worth as a Phase 2 addition to A.
