Growth Ecosystem — 3 Approaches

Where does real growth happen after onboarding? Each approach prioritizes differently.

A

Daily Loop Enhanced

Philosophy: The 5-minute daily session IS the growth engine. Make it deeper, not wider.

What changes:
  • Morning: Yesterday's Reflection → Learn → Implement (as today)
  • Evening: Peter asks about today's micro-action conversationally. Stores response as growth data.
  • Next morning: Peter weaves yesterday's practice outcome into today's content
  • Weekly: Peter delivers a "Weekly Mirror" — a short narrative of the week's patterns
  • Growth Map: Simple view accessible from dashboard showing journey arc + key moments
✓ Pros: Minimal new surfaces. Deepens what works. Low build effort.
✗ Cons: Growth Map may feel thin. No space for spontaneous reflection outside sessions.
B

Growth Journal + Mirror

Philosophy: The daily session plants seeds. The journal is where growth actually takes root.

What changes:
  • Daily session: Enhanced with evening practice check-in (same as A)
  • Growth Journal: New surface — a persistent, scrollable journal where Peter and the user have captured key moments, breakthroughs, and pattern shifts
  • Growth Mirror: Rich narrative view that tells the user's growth story — emotional arcs, practice patterns, relationship shifts — woven into one coherent narrative
  • Peter can prompt outside sessions: "I noticed something about your last few reflections — want to explore it?"
✓ Pros: Creates genuine depth. Users see themselves changing. Most therapeutic value.
✗ Cons: More surfaces to build. Risk of feeling overwhelming for "just 5 minutes a day" promise.
C

Living Dashboard (Recommended)

Philosophy: The dashboard IS the growth mirror. It evolves as you do.

What changes:
  • Daily session: Enhanced with evening practice check-in (same as A)
  • Dashboard evolves: Instead of a static hub, the dashboard itself reflects the user's growth story. Cards appear, shift, and deepen over time.
  • Peter's Notebook: A section on the dashboard (expandable to full view) where Peter keeps a running narrative — "Week 2: I'm noticing you're more willing to sit with discomfort..."
  • Growth Thread: Tap any dashboard card to see the story behind it — the pattern, the shift, the evidence from your sessions
  • Journey Arc: Visual progression through your journey that shows not just day count but thematic depth
✓ Pros: No new "places to go" — growth is visible where you already look. Feels alive. Most distinctive.
✗ Cons: Dashboard complexity grows. Need to keep it feeling warm, not data-heavy.

Why I recommend C (Living Dashboard)

The "relationship gym" metaphor works here — when you walk into a gym, you don't go to a separate "progress room." You see your growth in the mirrors, in the weights you pick up, in how the space feels different over time. The dashboard should work the same way. Growth shouldn't be something you go look at — it should be something you can't help but notice.

Option C also respects the "5 minutes a day" promise. The user opens the app, sees evidence of their growth on the dashboard, does their session, and leaves. No extra surfaces to visit unless they want to go deeper. But the depth is always there.
