**Expand the voice rules** to include:

- Prefer warm invitation over coaching pressure.
- Favor delight, ease, and relationship warmth over therapeutic gravity.
- Keep playful copy light, safe, and never mocking.
- Write for solo or couple use without making solo users feel behind.
- **NEW:** Use "you" and "your" — never "users" or "couples." The app speaks directly to one person.
- **NEW:** Presuppose forward movement. Write "When you notice..." not "If you decide to try..."
- **NEW:** Never use clinical language: avoid "exercise," "intervention," "module," "assessment," "compliance." Use "practice," "moment," "step," "reflection," "check-in" instead.
- **NEW:** Keep copy at fourth-grade reading level. Short sentences. Concrete words. No jargon.
- **NEW:** Error and empty states are gentle, never alarming. The person is safe. The app will catch up.

**Add a "Forbidden Copy Patterns" list:**

| Pattern | Why | Alternative |
|---------|-----|-------------|
| "Complete your exercise" | Clinical, pushy | "Your practice is ready" |
| "You haven't done X yet" | Guilt-driven | "Whenever you're ready" |
| "Track your progress" | Dashboard language | "See how far you've come" |
| "Rate your partner" | Evaluative, unsafe | Never — remove any rating mechanic between partners |
| "Submit" on forms | Cold, transactional | "Save" or "Continue" |
| "Loading..." | Generic tech | "Getting things ready..." or no text (just Peter animation) |
