# Phase 12 Product Definition

Date: 2026-03-31
Phase: Playful Connection MVP Definition

## Best Expansion Lane

Playful connection layer

## Why This Lane Wins Now

- Highest user delight:
  - It adds warmth, surprise, and ease faster than any other lane.
- Strongest retention impact for smallest scope:
  - Playful prompts are easier to open and repeat than deeper ritual or repair features.
- Lowest risk to the proven primary path:
  - It can live beside the existing serious-core experience without replacing it.
- Smallest buildable slice with real value:
  - Even one good playful prompt per day creates a new reason to open Sparq.

## What Not To Build Yet

### Shared rituals layer

Do not build first.

Why:
- Strong option, but it asks for a higher level of repeated commitment.
- Better as the next milestone after playful connection proves lighter repeat opens.

### Adaptive date-night layer

Do not build yet.

Why:
- Valuable, but more dependent on recommendation quality and logistics.
- Higher risk of feeling generic if launched too early.

### Couple game mode

Do not build yet.

Why:
- Largest scope and biggest identity risk.
- Too easy to become novelty instead of relationship depth.

### Joy and celebration layer

Do not build first.

Why:
- It becomes stronger after Sparq creates more playful and positive moments worth celebrating.

## Smallest MVP

### Working name

Daily Spark

### Core idea

One playful, emotionally smart connection prompt per day.

The prompt should feel:
- light
- personal
- easy to act on
- optional

It should work for:
- solo users who want to show up better
- partnered users who want a small shared moment

### MVP interaction

Each spark has:
- one short prompt
- one short explanation line
- simple action choices

Action choices:
- try it
- send it
- swap it

### Example prompt types

- flirt
- appreciation
- curiosity
- tiny challenge
- shared memory

### Example prompts

- “Send one text that says what you still like about them.”
- “Ask one fun question tonight: what made you laugh this week?”
- “Share one tiny memory you still smile about.”
- “Say one thing you notice that they may not know you see.”

## Exact Surfaces It Would Touch

### Dashboard

Add:
- one optional `Today’s Spark` card

Role:
- gives users a light reason to open the app
- keeps the primary serious path intact

### Daily-growth home

Add:
- one optional playful prompt below the main daily step

Role:
- supports users who want a lighter action alongside the core path
- must not replace the serious daily loop

### Reminder surface

Add:
- light reminder or nudge for the daily spark

Role:
- creates casual re-entry

### Partner share surface

Add:
- simple share or send action when a prompt is partner-ready

Role:
- turns prompts into real-world connection

### Not In MVP

Do not include yet:
- history
- favorites
- packs
- unlock trees
- scoring systems
- full game mechanics

## Guardrails

- Do not redesign the proven signup-driven primary path.
- Do not make the spark mandatory.
- Do not let playful prompts feel generic, cheesy, or adolescent.
- Do not assume active partner participation on every prompt.
- Keep the tone warm, emotionally aware, and simple.
- The spark should support real-life connection, not become content for content’s sake.

## Success Metrics

### Primary success

- more users open Sparq on days without active conflict
- dashboard engagement increases without hurting daily-loop completion
- spark interaction rate is meaningfully above passive card view rate

### Behavior success

- users choose `try it` or `send it`
- users return the next day after using a spark
- partnered and solo users both engage with the feature

### Guardrail success

- no drop in primary-path completion
- no confusion about the core daily step
- no increase in support or feedback showing the app feels less serious or less trustworthy

## Main Risks

### Tone risk

If prompts feel shallow or cheesy, Sparq loses credibility.

### Identity risk

If this becomes too game-like, Sparq may feel like a different product.

### Solo-first risk

If too many prompts assume a responsive partner, solo users will feel left out.

### Product clutter risk

If the spark is too prominent, it can distract from the serious core.

## Recommendation

The next product milestone should define and then build:
- one daily spark
- one lightweight dashboard surface
- one optional daily-growth companion surface
- one simple share/send action

That is the smallest version with real user value and the best chance of increasing delight and retention without risking the proven primary path.
