# Conflict & Repair Questions

---

## Easy

1. What's a small disagreement we've had that makes you laugh now? <!-- Positive Psychology -->
2. How do you usually prefer to cool off after a tough conversation? <!-- Emotion-Focused Therapy -->
3. When you're upset, what's one thing I can say that usually helps you feel a little better? <!-- Emotionally Focused Couples Therapy -->
4. If our arguments had a soundtrack, what funny song would play? <!-- Humor Therapy -->
5. What's a silly "argument" we've had that wasn't really serious? <!-- Positive Psychology -->
6. How do you like to receive an apology? <!-- Gottman Method -->
7. What is one "secret signal" we could use in public if one of us feels uncomfortable? <!-- Attachment Theory -->
8. Would you rather solve a disagreement with rock-paper-scissors or a dance-off? <!-- Play Therapy -->
9. What's one way I can show you I'm truly listening during a disagreement? <!-- Active Listening (Rogerian) -->
10. How can we make arguments feel less scary for each other? <!-- Trauma-Informed Care -->

---

## Medium

11. When you were growing up, how did your family handle conflict? <!-- Family Systems Theory -->
12. Is there a way you like to reconnect after a hard conversation? <!-- Emotionally Focused Couples Therapy -->
13. What do you think makes a good apology? <!-- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) -->
14. When you're upset, do you prefer comfort, space, or solutions? <!-- Emotionally Focused Couples Therapy -->
15. How can I make you feel more emotionally safe during disagreements? <!-- Attachment Theory -->
16. What "repair attempt" (like a joke, hug, etc.) usually works best for you? <!-- Gottman Method -->
17. What's one way we can signal to each other that a fight isn't about "winning"? <!-- Nonviolent Communication -->
18. How do you know when an argument is actually about something deeper? <!-- Internal Family Systems (IFS) -->
19. If you could change one thing about how we resolve conflicts, what would it be? <!-- Solution-Focused Brief Therapy -->
20. What's one phrase or word that immediately helps de-escalate tension for you? <!-- Crisis Intervention -->
21. Is there a "safe word" or silly code we could use if things start feeling overwhelming? <!-- Play Therapy -->
22. What does forgiveness mean to you in a relationship? <!-- Positive Psychology -->
23. How does your body usually feel during conflict? (e.g., tense, numb, energized?) <!-- Somatic Experiencing -->
24. What's one thing you admire about how I handle conflict? <!-- Strengths-Based Therapy -->
25. How do you usually know you're ready to move on after a disagreement? <!-- Self-Compassion Therapy -->

---

## Hard

26. What's a recurring conflict that we haven't fully healed from yet? <!-- Gottman Method -->
27. What deeper fear or need tends to come up for you during arguments? <!-- Attachment Theory -->
28. How can we better recognize when one of us is emotionally flooded? <!-- Gottman Method -->
29. What is the hardest part about being vulnerable during conflict? <!-- Vulnerability Research (Brené Brown) -->
30. What's one conflict from our past you wish we had handled differently? <!-- Narrative Therapy -->
31. When you're very hurt, what's one thing you wish I understood without you needing to explain? <!-- Emotion-Focused Therapy -->
32. What's something I unintentionally do during conflict that hurts you? <!-- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) -->
33. How have your childhood experiences shaped the way you handle disagreements today? <!-- Family Systems Theory -->
34. When you feel misunderstood during a conflict, what would help you feel seen? <!-- Nonviolent Communication -->
35. What's a "story" you sometimes tell yourself about me during a fight that's not always true? <!-- Narrative Therapy -->
36. How can we create a ritual for repairing after conflict (e.g., a hug, a walk)? <!-- Ritual Theory -->
37. What's your biggest fear when it comes to arguing with me? <!-- Attachment Theory -->
38. What is something you need from me during a major conflict that you don't always get? <!-- Emotion-Focused Therapy -->
39. How can I better hold space for your feelings even when we disagree? <!-- Compassion-Focused Therapy -->
40. What belief about conflict do you think holds you back in our relationship? <!-- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) -->

---

## Deepest

41. When we argue, do you ever feel a fear of losing me? <!-- Attachment Theory -->
42. How can we grow stronger through our disagreements instead of growing apart? <!-- Strengths-Based Therapy -->
43. What wound from your past do you think sometimes gets triggered in our conflicts? <!-- Trauma-Informed Care -->
44. What do you most need me to believe about you when we're not getting along? <!-- Emotion-Focused Therapy -->
45. How can we honor each other's pain even when we don't fully understand it? <!-- Compassion-Focused Therapy -->
46. When we repair after a fight, what makes you feel truly close again? <!-- Emotionally Focused Couples Therapy -->
47. How would you want us to model conflict repair if we were teaching other couples? <!-- Gottman Method -->
48. What's the most healing thing someone could say to you after a painful argument? <!-- Self-Compassion Therapy -->
49. If you could write a "conflict survival guide" just for us, what would the first rule be? <!-- Solution-Focused Brief Therapy -->
50. In your dream version of our relationship, what does conflict look and feel like? <!-- Positive Psychology -->
