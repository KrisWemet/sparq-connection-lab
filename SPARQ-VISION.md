# SPARQ_VISION.md

> "Stronger individuals create stronger relationships. Stronger relationships create stronger families. Stronger families create stronger communities."

## Core Philosophy
- **Individual First:** A self-improvement tool for the relationship context. We focus on changing the *user's* behaviors and thoughts, not fixing their partner.
- **Adaptive & Relational:** The app feels like a friend (Peter the Otter), not a clinician. It learns the user's profile silently through natural conversation.
- **Goal:** 2,500 active paying users in Year 1.

## The "Trojan Horse" Onboarding (Days 1-14)
- **No Clinical Quizzes:** We do not ask "What is your attachment style?"
- **Silent Profiling:** The AI analyzes daily journal entries to infer Attachment Style, Love Language, and Conflict Style.
- **Daily Loop:**
  1.  **Morning Story:** A relatable scenario (e.g., "Sarah and Tom") teaching a concept without jargon.
  2.  **Daily Action:** A small, specific task to practice that concept.
  3.  **Evening Reflection:** A check-in on how it went.

## The Skill Tree (Day 15+)
- **Gamified Progression:** Users unlock categories (Communication, Conflict, Intimacy).
- **Leveled Content:** Basic → Advanced → Expert. Users must master Basic to unlock Advanced.
- **Monetization:** Natural upsells that feel like "next steps" rather than "paywalls" (e.g., "You've mastered the basics! Ready for the Advanced Conflict Toolkit?").

## The Voice (Peter the Otter)
- **Tone:** Warm, 4th-grade reading level, non-clinical.
- **Feedback Style:**
  - **Baseline:** Celebration of Effort (Positive Psychology).
  - **Adaptive:** Shifts to Radical Acceptance (Comfort) or Gentle Accountability (Coaching) based on the user's emotional state.

## Key Features
- **The Translator:** Helps partners rephrase messages based on the other's profile (e.g., "Don't say X to an Avoidant partner; say Y.").
- **Conflict First Aid:** A panic button for real-time de-escalation.
- **Growth Dashboard:** Tracks skill acquisition (e.g., "Active Listening Level 2"), not just usage stats.
